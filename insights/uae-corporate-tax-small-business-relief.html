Small Business Relief under UAE Corporate Tax: who actually qualifies

Taher Furniturewala · Tax Associate · 2026

One of the most common questions I get from small business owners filing their first UAE Corporate Tax return is whether they actually need to pay anything at all. For many, the answer hinges on a single relief: Small Business Relief.

The basic rule

A resident taxable person can elect to be treated as having no taxable income — effectively a 0% Corporate Tax outcome — for a tax period if their revenue for that period, and every prior tax period since UAE CT came into effect, has not exceeded AED 3,000,000. This isn't a profit test; it's a revenue test, so even a business with thin margins needs to check turnover, not net income, against the threshold.

It's an election, not automatic

Small Business Relief has to be claimed on the Corporate Tax return — it doesn't apply by default just because you're under the threshold. That means the decision to elect (or not) should be made deliberately each filing period, not assumed.

What you give up by electing

This is the part that catches people out. If you elect into Small Business Relief, you generally can't claim certain other CT benefits for that period — including tax loss relief and foreign tax credits. For a business sitting on losses it wants to carry forward, or one with foreign tax already paid, electing in isn't automatically the right call even if revenue qualifies.

Who's excluded

The relief is not available to Qualifying Free Zone Persons claiming the 0% Free Zone regime, or to members of large multinational groups within scope of the OECD Pillar Two rules. If your business sits in a free zone or is part of a larger group structure, the analysis changes.

The practical takeaway

Before electing, I run clients through three checks: (1) is revenue — not profit — actually under AED 3,000,000 for this period and every period since CT applied to you, (2) do you have losses or foreign tax credits worth preserving instead, and (3) does your structure (free zone status, group membership) disqualify you outright. Get those three right, and the relief decision becomes straightforward.

This is a general overview, not advice for your specific position — UAE CT rules and thresholds can be updated by the Ministry of Finance and FTA. If you want a proper read on whether Small Business Relief makes sense for your business, reach out and I'll walk through it with you.
